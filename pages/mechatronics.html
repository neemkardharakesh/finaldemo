Mechatronics

The mechatronic system used to run actuators with your computer can be divided into three primary components:

  • Computer: NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano (19V, 1–3.5A) or Raspberry Pi (5V)
  • Actuator: CubeMars AK-Series (24V–48V), CyberGear Micromotor (24V), or RobStride Actuator (24V)
  • Batteries: Unified power source or separate power sources for computer and actuators

Power System

The power system provides energy to both the onboard computer and actuators. Proper wiring and safety protection are critical for reliable operation.

Required Components

  • 12 Gauge Black Wire
  • 12 Gauge Red Wire
  • XT30 or XT30 (2+2) Connectors
  • Emergency Stop Switch (E-Stop)
  • Fuse Holder
  • 20A Fuse
  • Buck Converter
  • Power Bank Connection Cable
  • Battery Mount Adapter

Power - Battery Connections

Multiple battery configurations are supported depending on the battery type and whether the computer shares power with the actuator system.

LiPo Battery Setup

  • Use a Female XT60 Connector.
  • Solder Red Wire to Positive Terminal.
  • Solder Black Wire to Negative Terminal.

Drill Battery Setup

  • Use a Drill Battery Adapter.
  • Connect Red Wire to Positive Output.
  • Connect Black Wire to Ground Output.

Additional Components

Depending on the configuration, additional components may be required:

  • 12 Gauge Black and Red Wire
  • Buck Converter
  • Fuse Holder and Fuse
  • Computer Power Cable
  • Barrel Jack Connector
  • Micro-USB Connector
  • USB-C Connector

Buck Converter Configuration

The output voltage of the buck converter must match the required voltage of your computer.

Device Required Voltage
NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano 19V
Raspberry Pi 5V

Connect the input side of the converter to the battery. Connect a multimeter to the output side and adjust the potentiometer using a Phillips-head screwdriver until the desired voltage is reached.

Safety Guidelines

⚠ Important

Always install a fuse and emergency stop switch before connecting actuators to the battery system.

Verify polarity before powering the system. Reversed polarity may permanently damage electronics.

Confirm buck converter output voltage using a multimeter before connecting the computer.

Verification Checklist

  • Battery connected correctly.
  • Fuse installed.
  • Emergency Stop connected.
  • Buck Converter adjusted correctly.
  • Computer powers on successfully.
  • Actuator receives correct voltage.
  • CAN communication operational.
